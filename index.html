FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is ABA therapy?

Applied Behavior Analysis (ABA) is a scientifically validated approach to understanding behavior and how it is affected by the environment. ABA therapy focuses on teaching essential skills, reducing challenging behaviors, and increasing social, communication, and adaptive functioning.

Who qualifies for ABA services?

We serve children, youth, and young adults ages 2–21 diagnosed with autism spectrum disorder (ASD) or other developmental disabilities. A formal autism diagnosis from a licensed professional is typically required for insurance authorization.

How long are therapy sessions?

Session length varies based on your child's individualized treatment plan and insurance authorization. Typically, sessions range from 2 to 4 hours for BHT-ABA individual services. BCBA supervision visits are scheduled regularly throughout the month.

Where do services take place?

We offer flexible service locations including your child's home, school, our center, and community settings. Location is determined based on your child's treatment goals and what environment best supports their learning.

How do I get started?

Getting started is easy. Contact us via phone or our online form. Our team will verify your insurance, discuss your child's needs, and schedule an initial assessment with a BCBA. The assessment is completed within 15 days of starting services.

Do you accept insurance?

Yes! We accept Aetna, Geisinger Health Plan, and Highmark of Pennsylvania. Pennsylvania mandates insurance coverage for ABA therapy for autism. Our team will verify your specific benefits and guide you through the authorization process at no cost to you.
